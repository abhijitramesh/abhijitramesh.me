The arc

I spent five years leading ML at Theta Tech AI and TexNano — segmentation models for endoscopic ultrasound, training systems for an FDA-pathway cardiac diagnostic device, and the inference services clinicians used at the point of care. The work shipped. Models live in clinical use today.

In late 2025 I left to dig into the systems layer where the model actually runs. I joined Tyler Sorensen’s Concurrency and Heterogeneous Programming Lab at UCSC, alongside PhD student Reese Levine, to write the WebGPU backend for llama.cpp. Three PRs merged in Q1 2026 across shader compilation, dispatch limits, and kernel migration. I built the cross-browser benchmark the contributor community uses to validate them.

On any given day: WGSL spec edge cases, kernel autotuning, and a coffee in Santa Cruz.
