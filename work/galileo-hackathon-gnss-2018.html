Problem

Insurance companies struggle to accurately assess customer risk profiles. Traditional credibility scoring ignores real-world driving behavior — specifically whether a driver routinely travels through high-risk or hazardous terrain.

Contribution

As part of a team, built an Android application for the satellite-navigation hackathon that used GPS/GNSS location data to track automobile routes and identify frequent traversal of dangerous terrain. The derived risk signal was surfaced to insurance companies as a credibility/risk score input.

Technical details

Used Android's location/GPS APIs and GNSS satellite data to log and analyze route patterns. Risk assessment logic flagged routes against terrain danger heuristics.

Outcome

First hackathon participation. No prize awarded. Introduced GPS-based sensing and location-data processing as a practical domain.
